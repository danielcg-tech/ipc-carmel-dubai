Church History

Established in Dubai · Indian Pentecostal Church of God

IPC Carmel Dubai was founded in the late 1990s by a small but fervent group of Malayalam-speaking believers who had migrated to the United Arab Emirates in search of livelihood and opportunity. Meeting initially in private homes and rented halls, these pioneers carried with them a deep-rooted Pentecostal faith nurtured in the churches of Kerala, and a burning desire to worship the Lord in spirit and in truth far from their homeland.

"For where two or three are gathered in my name, there am I among them." — Matthew 18:20

The congregation was formally registered under the Indian Pentecostal Church of God (IPC) in the early 2000s, aligning with the wider global IPC fellowship that traces its roots to the great Pentecostal revival that swept through Kerala in the early twentieth century. Under its first resident pastor, the assembly grew steadily, drawing together families from various districts of Kerala — Kottayam, Ernakulam, Thrissur, and beyond — who found in IPC Carmel a spiritual home away from home.

Over the years, the church has expanded its ministry to encompass a vibrant Sunday School, a dynamic youth fellowship (YPF), a women's ministry, and regular intercessory prayer groups. Special conventions, Christmas and Easter celebrations, and annual Thanksgiving services have become cherished traditions that knit the community together and provide moments of collective praise and renewal.

Today, IPC Carmel Dubai stands as a growing assembly of believers committed to the Great Commission, to the nurture of families in the faith, and to the spiritual well-being of the Malayalam-speaking community in Dubai. The church continues to uphold the foundational Pentecostal convictions of the new birth, the baptism of the Holy Spirit, divine healing, and the imminent return of our Lord Jesus Christ.

We give glory to God for every milestone and look forward with faith to the chapters yet to be written in the story of IPC Carmel Dubai.
